Working through this assignment gave me a much clearer sense of how encapsulation actually shapes the way I write code. At first it felt like just another rule, but once I started connecting the Engine, Car, Passenger, and Train classes, I realized how much cleaner and more predictable everything becomes when each class protects its own data. I couldn’t just reach in and change things directly; I had to use the methods each class provided, and that forced me to think more intentionally about design. Some parts of the assignment went smoothly, especially once the structure clicked, but matching the interfaces exactly was harder than I expected — even small differences like `boolean` vs. `Boolean` caused big errors. If I were giving advice to someone taking this course later, I’d tell them to read the interfaces carefully, test their code often with a simple main method, and not panic when Java throws long error messages. Most of the time the problem is just one mismatched method signature. Overall, this assignment helped me understand why encapsulation matters and how it keeps larger programs organized and easier to debug.
